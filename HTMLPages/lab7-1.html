Who is Ada Lovelace?

The daughter of famed poet lord byron Augusya Ada Byron, Countless of LoveLace- better know as "Ada LoveLace" - was born in london on december 10, 1815. Ada showed her gift for mathematics at an early age. She transfered an article on an intervention by charles Babbage, and added her own comments. Because she introduced many computer concepts, Ada is considered the first computer programmer. Ada died November 27,1852.
Legacy

Ada LoveLave contributions to the field of computers science were not discovered until the 1950s. Her notes were reintoduced to the world by B.V Bowden, who republished them in Faster Than A Thought: A Symposium on Digital Computing Machines in 1953. Since then, Ada has recived many posthumous honors for her work. In 1980, The U.S. department of Defense named a newly developed computer language "Ada" after Lovelace
